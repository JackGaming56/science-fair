Science Fair Free Energy Waterwheel Project

©Copyright 2026 Mohamed Abdullah. Website, Video, and Qrcode are apart of this copyright.

Research Question

Could the water wheel produce electricity by water flowing and spinning it?

Scientific Concept

The Water flows from the bottle (using a straw) to the wheel, which spins and moves the rubber band around which is connected from the wheel to a motor, the motor turns that kinetic energy to electricity which powers the LED (the LED has a battery that powers it a little bit as the motor doesn't produce many volts). As all of this happens, there is a water pump taking all the water back from the plastic tank to the bottle to repeat.

STEM Connection

Science (Physics), Technology (Measurement & Tools), Engineering (Design), Mathematics (Calculation & Analysis)

Hypothesis

If the water is flowing on the wheel and the wheel spins fast enough, It should produce electricity by the motor to the LED

Procedure

First, We took some ice cream sticks and cut them in half. Next, We glued these half sticks to a bottle cap to create a wheel pattern. Then, We glued another bottle cap on the other side and glued bottle caps on the front of each stick. After that, We poked a hole in the middle of the bottle caps to insert a stick inside and glue it there. Then, We got ice cream sticks to hold the structure from both sides so we could stick it onto the plastic tank. Next, We took some sticks to make a tower sort of structure to balance the water bottle on top of it, then the bottle flows water onto the wheel to spin the wheel so it could power the motor that we placed after. Next, We add a water pump that sucks up all the water in the plastic tank. Lastly, We added an LED, a switch, and a battery that powers half the LED because the motor doesn't produce many volts. Independent Variable Water Flow: The speed of water falling from the bottle onto the wheel. Dependent Variable Voltage/Energy Output: How much power the motor makes to light the LED. Control Variables Wheel Design: The number of sticks and bottle caps used. The Tank: The flat, big plastic bowl used to catch the water. The Hardware: Using the same motor, LED, and battery for every test.

Results

Water flows, wheel spins and the motor produces electricity which lights up the LED
